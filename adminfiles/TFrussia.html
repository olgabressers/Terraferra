Russian Designers on TF — US Sanctions
Research as of June 2026.
Conclusion
Not prohibited. Fashion is not a sanctioned sector. Consumer marketplace services are outside the IT prohibition scope.
Legal requirements — must do
1.Screen every counterparty against the OFAC SDN list before onboarding and before each payout. SDN = Specially Designated Nationals — OFAC's list of individuals and entities that US persons and companies are prohibited by law from transacting with. Penalty applies even if you did not know the person was on the list.
2.Do not provide brand strategy, AI advisory, or analytics features to Russia-located users. EO 14071 prohibits providing management consulting and business intelligence software services to persons located in Russia. Violation is a civil and criminal offence.
3.Verify beneficial ownership for any legal entity counterparty. Under OFAC's 50% rule, a company is treated as a blocked entity if 50% or more of it is owned by a person on the SDN list — even if the company itself is not listed.
01Applicable Sanctions Programs
Program Scope Covers TF fashion marketplace?
EO 14024 (31 CFR §587) Blocks designated individuals and entities in named sectors: financial services, technology, defense, aerospace, electronics, metals, energy, manufacturing, management consulting, architecture, engineering. OFAC EO 14024 program page ↗ Fashion not designated. Non-designated Russian individuals are not blocked.
EO 14071 — Prohibited Services US persons cannot provide: accounting, management consulting, architecture, engineering, trust/corporate formation, IT consultancy, IT support and cloud-based services for enterprise management software and design/manufacturing software to persons in Russia. OFAC FAQ 1128 ↗ Consumer marketplace = outside scope. Management consulting / brand advisory features = legal risk (see §03).
EO 13685 (Crimea) Imports from / exports to / investments in Crimea. Not relevant to a digital platform with no Crimea-specific nexus.
SSI List (Sectoral Sanctions) Debt / equity transactions with major Russian financial and energy companies on the SSI list. Not relevant. TF is not providing debt, equity, or financing to sanctioned entities.
GL 25F (Russia internet services) General License explicitly authorizing internet communications services to Russia: social platforms, collaboration tools, web hosting, user authentication. Federal Register — GL 25F (Sep 2024) ↗ Protects TF's communications and platform-access layer.
02IT Services Prohibition — Does It Apply?

EO 14071 (effective September 12, 2024) prohibits US persons from providing cloud-based services to Russia for enterprise management software (ERP, CRM, business intelligence, supply chain, project management, data warehousing) and design/manufacturing software (CAD, CAM, BIM, engineer-to-order tools). OFAC FAQs 1184 & 1188 ↗

TF Feature Prohibited Category? Assessment
Consumer marketplace (pre-orders, co-creation, drops) Not listed Outside prohibition scope. Consumer e-commerce platforms are not enterprise management or manufacturing software. DLA Piper analysis ↗ · Arnold & Porter ↗
Influencer / ambassador payout layer Not listed Outside scope. Financial services prohibition applies to Russian banks, not payment disbursement by a platform.
Sales analytics / performance dashboards Potentially "enterprise management" Arguable — business intelligence tools are in the prohibited list. Get legal opinion before shipping analytics to Russia-located designers. Miller & Chevalier overview ↗
Brand strategy tools / launch guidance / AI insights to designers Potentially "management consulting" EO 14071 prohibits "management consulting" broadly, including strategic business advice and brand management. Any feature providing strategic advisory output to a Russia-located designer falls in the prohibited zone under EO 14071. Davis Polk — EO 14071 guidance ↗
03SDN Screening — Mandatory for Every Counterparty

OFAC sanctions are strict liability. Transacting with an SDN — even unknowingly — triggers civil penalties. OFAC SDN FAQ ↗

Requirement Detail
Screen at onboarding Every designer, influencer, or brand must be checked against the OFAC SDN list before approval.
Screen before each payout Designations change multiple times weekly. A clean screen at onboarding does not substitute for screening at payment time.
50% ownership rule A non-listed entity is still blocked if 50%+ of its ownership is held by a blocked person, even if the entity itself is not on the SDN list.
On hit: block and report Freeze the transaction immediately. File a blocked property report with OFAC within 10 business days.
Screening tools Free search: sanctionssearch.ofac.treas.gov ↗. Commercial automated screening (Dow Jones, Refinitiv, Sanction Scanner) recommended for platform-scale volume.
04Payment Infrastructure — The Practical Blocker
Route Status
Visa / Mastercard to Russian-issued cards Non-functional. Both suspended Russia operations March 2022. Chip certificates for existing cards expired January 2025. Visa press release ↗
SWIFT transfers to Russia Major Russian banks removed from SWIFT from March 2022. Most Western correspondent banks refuse Russia-bound transfers regardless of SWIFT connectivity.
Wise / Payoneer Both have severely restricted Russia service as of 2025. Not a reliable route.
Payment via non-Russian account (Georgian, Armenian, EU bank) Legally and technically feasible if the account is outside Russia and the holder is not SDN-listed. Used by European payroll intermediaries working with Russian contractors. Requires each designer to hold a non-Russian bank account.
Crypto Not prohibited per se for non-SDN individuals, but raises AML/BSA compliance obligations. OFAC has warned about crypto use to evade sanctions. Requires separate legal analysis.
05Compliance Paths — Ranked by Simplicity
Path A · Cleanest
Russian diaspora / EU or US-resident designers
A Russian national legally resident in the EU, US, or other non-sanctioned country is not "a person located in the Russian Federation" for EO 14071 purposes. No special license required. Payments go to a non-Russian account. SDN screening still required on the individual.
Path B · Clean
Require non-Russian legal entity
Designer registers a legal entity in the EU, UAE, UK, or other third country and contracts through it. TF's counterparty is that entity — not a Russia-located individual. Resolves both the OFAC and the payment problem.
Path C · Workable
Require non-Russian bank account
Russia-resident designer contracts with TF but receives all payouts into a bank account outside Russia (Georgian, Armenian, or EU account). Sidesteps payment infrastructure. OFAC SDN screening of the individual still required.
Path D · Fallback
OFAC Specific License
For any scenario that remains ambiguous after legal review, apply to OFAC for a specific license. OFAC grants these for commercially reasonable cases outside the prohibited categories. Process takes weeks to months. OFAC licensing overview ↗
Mandatory baseline for all paths: SDN screening at onboarding and before every payout. "We didn't know they were on the list" does not eliminate civil liability under strict liability enforcement.
06Key Risks to Watch
Risk Why it matters Action
"Management consulting" prohibition Defined broadly to include strategic business advice and brand management. Any TF feature providing strategic advisory output to a Russia-located designer could be argued to fall under EO 14071. Davis Polk — EO 14071 services scope ↗ Get written legal opinion before shipping AI-driven brand insights or launch strategy tools to Russia-located users.
Sanctions change frequently New sector determinations were added in December 2025 and January 2025. The framework evolves; an analysis current today may be outdated in 6 months. Build a periodic review cycle. Track updates at ofac.treasury.gov ↗
50% ownership / nominee structures Designers may route through third-country entities controlled by SDN-listed individuals. Name screening alone does not catch this. KYC must include beneficial ownership disclosure for any legal entity counterparty.
Secondary sanctions OFAC has expanded secondary sanctions targeting non-US financial institutions that support Russian sanctions evasion. Affects which payment processors will cooperate with TF. Non-issue if TF uses compliant Western payment infrastructure. Do not route via Russian payment processors.
07SDN Screening — Technology & Integration

The SDN list is a machine-readable database maintained by OFAC. It can be integrated directly into a platform's onboarding and payout flows.

Option What it is Integration
OFAC free download OFAC publishes the full SDN list as downloadable XML, CSV, and XLSX files, updated in near-real-time. treasury.gov/ofac/downloads ↗ Can be ingested into any database and matched against counterparty names/IDs. Requires building the matching logic in-house. Free.
OFAC Sanctions List Service API OFAC provides a free REST API to query the SDN list by name, ID, or other identifiers. sanctionslist.ofac.treas.gov ↗ REST API calls. Can be triggered at onboarding and before each payout. Free. No SLA or uptime guarantee from OFAC — not suitable as the sole production screening method for a live platform.
ComplyAdvantage Commercial sanctions and AML screening platform. Covers SDN list plus 1,000+ other global sanctions lists. Real-time database updates. complyadvantage.com ↗ REST API. Supports batch screening at onboarding and event-triggered screening before payouts. Usage-based pricing (per-search). Built for marketplace and fintech platforms.
Sanction Scanner Sanctions, PEP, and adverse media screening. Covers OFAC SDN plus 3,000+ global lists. sanctionscanner.com ↗ REST API with webhook support for ongoing monitoring. Can alert when a previously clean user appears on a new list.
Dow Jones Risk & Compliance Enterprise-grade screening covering SDN, PEP, and adverse media. Used by banks and large payment platforms. dowjones.com/professional/risk ↗ API + batch file processing. Enterprise pricing. Better suited to high-volume platforms than early-stage.
Persona Identity verification platform with built-in watchlist screening (including OFAC SDN). Commonly used for marketplace KYC flows. withpersona.com ↗ SDK + API. Drop-in onboarding flow with document verification + SDN check combined. Practical for a fashion marketplace that needs KYC at designer onboarding.
Integration point on TF platform When to trigger What to check
Designer / influencer onboarding Before account approval Full name, date of birth, country, any linked legal entity name against SDN list. Beneficial ownership disclosure for entities.
Before each payout At payment initiation Re-screen payee name + bank account country. List updates multiple times weekly — a clean check at onboarding is not sufficient.
Ongoing monitoring (optional but recommended) Automated, triggered by list updates Commercial vendors (ComplyAdvantage, Sanction Scanner) offer webhooks that alert when an existing user appears on a newly updated list. Catches mid-relationship designations.
08Russia-Based Consumers & Influencers
User type Platform access Payments Physical goods
Consumers in Russia Browsing, discovery, and social features are covered by GL 25F. Platform access is not restricted. Paying from Russia: Visa/Mastercard non-functional to Russian-issued cards since 2022. Russian consumers cannot pay via standard card rails. No legal prohibition on accepting payment from a non-SDN Russian consumer — but the payment infrastructure does not support it. Physical goods cannot be shipped to Russia if they qualify as luxury goods under BIS rules. Fashion apparel, footwear, and accessories are explicitly listed. Federal Register — BIS luxury goods rule ↗
Influencers / ambassadors in Russia Platform access and content creation covered by GL 25F. Earning commissions = same payment problem as designers. Must have non-Russian bank account to receive payouts. SDN screening required before each payout. Not applicable — influencers receive cash commissions, not goods.
Russian nationals outside Russia Not subject to EO 14071. The prohibition applies to persons "located in the Russian Federation" — not to Russian nationals living in the EU, US, or other third countries. No payment infrastructure problem if they hold a non-Russian bank account. SDN screening still required. Standard shipping to their non-Russia address. BIS luxury goods restriction does not apply.
Practical conclusion
Russia-based consumers Can browse and engage with the platform. Cannot realistically complete a purchase: card payment is blocked and physical goods cannot be shipped to Russia. No action required to block them — the infrastructure gap prevents transactions automatically. SDN screening is still required if they attempt a purchase.
Russia-based influencers Can participate in content, social, and ambassador activities. Cannot receive commission payouts to a Russian account. Require a non-Russian bank account as a condition of payout — same rule as designers.
Digital-only goods (no physical shipment) If TF were ever to sell digital products (gift cards, tokens, digital downloads) to Russia-based users: no BIS restriction applies to digital goods. GL 25F covers digital content distribution. Payment remains the bottleneck.
09Russian Co-Founders, Investors & Shareholders

The determining factor for all ownership instruments — common shares, preferred shares, SAFE, convertible note — is whether the individual is on the OFAC SDN list. The instrument type does not change the rule. SDN-listed: blocked in all forms. Not SDN-listed: permitted in all forms.

Note on EO 14071 "new investment" prohibition: this restricts US persons from making new investments in Russia — it does not restrict a Russian national from investing money into a US company. A Russian investor wiring into a US Delaware C-corp SAFE is outside this prohibition. OFAC FAQ 1128 ↗

Situation Legal position What this means for TF
Russian national holds any ownership interest (shares, SAFE, note) — not on SDN list Permitted. No provision of US sanctions law prohibits a non-designated Russian national from holding an ownership interest in a US company, in any instrument form. OFAC EO 14024 program page ↗ Screen the individual against the SDN list at the time of grant or investment, and periodically thereafter. If they remain off the list, no restriction applies.
Russian national holds any ownership interest (shares, SAFE, note) — on SDN list Blocked in all instrument forms. Paying dividends, distributions, or any other benefit to an SDN-listed person is prohibited. If the SDN-listed person holds 50%+ of TF, TF itself becomes a blocked entity and any US person transacting with TF is also in violation. OFAC 50% rule ↗ No SDN-listed person can hold any form of ownership interest in TF. Existing interests must be restructured out before TF can operate legally.
Paying salary or consulting fees to a Russia-resident co-founder EO 14071 prohibits US persons from providing or receiving "management consulting" services when payment flows to or from a person located in Russia. Paying a Russia-resident co-founder for active management work falls in this grey zone. OFAC FAQ 1128 — EO 14071 services ↗ Risk arises only when payment flows for active work. A Russia-resident co-founder who holds equity but receives no salary or consulting fee is outside this prohibition.
Paying dividends or profit distributions to a Russia-resident shareholder (non-SDN) No US sanctions law prohibits distributing dividends to a non-SDN Russian-resident shareholder. The legal right to pay exists. Executing the payment does not: Visa/Mastercard and most SWIFT routes to Russia are non-functional since 2022. Shareholder agreement should require payment to a non-Russian bank account. This is a contractual requirement, not a sanctions requirement.
Required actions
Screen all co-founders and investors against the SDN list At equity grant or investment, at each subsequent funding round, and when OFAC updates the list. Applies to every shareholder regardless of nationality.
Apply the 50% rule to the cap table If any SDN-listed person holds or could accumulate 50%+ of TF equity, TF itself becomes a blocked entity. Cap table must be structured to prevent this outcome.
Separate passive equity from active services A Russia-resident co-founder who holds equity but provides no management services (no salary, no consulting agreement) avoids the EO 14071 management consulting risk.
