Russian Designers on TF — US Sanctions
Whether a US Delaware C-corp can legally operate a fashion marketplace with Russian-based designers. Research current as of June 2026. Not legal advice — requires US sanctions counsel sign-off before onboarding any Russian counterparty.
Bottom Line
Not structurally prohibited. Fashion is not a sanctioned sector. The platform's services are outside the IT software prohibition scope. Two mandatory requirements: SDN screening of every counterparty, and non-Russian banking for all payouts.
Sector Risk
Low
Fashion / consumer goods not designated under any Russia sanctions program.
IT Prohibition Risk
Watch
Consumer marketplace is outside scope. Brand analytics / advisory features require legal review.
Payment Risk
High
Visa/Mastercard and most SWIFT routes to Russia are non-functional. Non-Russian bank accounts required.
01Applicable Sanctions Programs
Program Scope Covers TF fashion marketplace?
EO 14024 (31 CFR §587) Blocks designated individuals and entities in named sectors: financial services, technology, defense, aerospace, electronics, metals, energy, manufacturing, management consulting, architecture, engineering. Fashion not designated. Non-designated Russian individuals are not blocked.
EO 14071 — Prohibited Services US persons cannot provide: accounting, management consulting, architecture, engineering, trust/corporate formation, IT consultancy, IT support and cloud-based services for enterprise management software and design/manufacturing software to persons in Russia. Consumer marketplace = outside scope. Management consulting / brand advisory features = legal risk (see §03).
EO 13685 (Crimea) Imports from / exports to / investments in Crimea. Not relevant to a digital platform with no Crimea-specific nexus.
SSI List (Sectoral Sanctions) Debt / equity transactions with major Russian financial and energy companies listed on the SSI list. Not relevant. TF is not providing debt, equity, or financing to sanctioned entities.
GL 25F (Russia internet services) General License explicitly authorizing internet communications services to Russia: social platforms, collaboration tools, web hosting, user authentication. Protects TF's communications and platform-access layer.
02IT Services Prohibition — Does It Apply?

EO 14071 (effective September 12, 2024) prohibits US persons from providing cloud-based services to Russia for enterprise management software (ERP, CRM, business intelligence, supply chain, project management, data warehousing) and design/manufacturing software (CAD, CAM, BIM, engineer-to-order tools).

TF Feature Prohibited Category? Assessment
Consumer marketplace (pre-orders, co-creation, drops) Not listed Outside prohibition scope. Consumer e-commerce platforms are not enterprise management or manufacturing software.
Influencer / ambassador payout layer Not listed Outside scope. Financial services prohibition applies to Russian banks, not payment disbursement by a platform.
Sales analytics / performance dashboards Potentially "enterprise management" Arguable. If framed as business intelligence software provided to a Russia-located entity, could be challenged. Get legal opinion before shipping analytics to Russian designers.
Brand strategy tools / launch guidance / AI insights to designers Potentially "management consulting" EO 14071 prohibits "management consulting" broadly, including strategic business advice and brand management. Any feature that provides strategic advisory output to a Russia-located designer needs legal sign-off.
03SDN Screening — Mandatory for Every Counterparty

OFAC sanctions are strict liability. Transacting with an SDN — even unknowingly — triggers civil penalties. Required steps:

Requirement Detail
Screen at onboarding Every designer, influencer, or brand seeking to join the platform must be checked against the OFAC SDN list before approval.
Screen before each payout Designations change (OFAC updates the SDN list multiple times weekly). A clean screen at onboarding does not substitute for screening at payment time.
50% ownership rule A non-listed entity is still blocked if 50% or more of its ownership is held by a blocked person, even if the entity itself is not on the SDN list.
On hit: block and report Freeze the transaction immediately. File a blocked property report with OFAC within 10 business days.
Tools Free: sanctionssearch.ofac.treas.gov. Commercial automated screening (Dow Jones, Refinitiv, Sanction Scanner) recommended for platform-scale volume.
04Payment Infrastructure — The Practical Blocker
Route Status
Visa / Mastercard to Russian-issued cards Non-functional. Both suspended Russia operations March 2022. Cards issued by Russian banks do not process on international networks. Chip certificates expired January 2025.
SWIFT transfers to Russia Major Russian banks removed from SWIFT from March 2022. Remaining connected banks cannot find Western correspondents willing to process. Practically blocked.
Wise / Payoneer Both have severely restricted Russia service. Not a reliable route as of 2025.
Payment via non-Russian account (Georgian, Armenian, EU bank) Legally and technically feasible if the account is outside Russia and the holder is not SDN-listed. Used by European payroll intermediaries. Requires each designer to set up a non-Russian bank account.
Crypto Not prohibited per se for non-SDN individuals, but raises its own AML/BSA compliance obligations. OFAC has warned about crypto use to evade sanctions. Requires separate legal analysis.
05Compliance Paths — Ranked by Simplicity
Path A · Cleanest
Russian diaspora / EU or US-resident designers
A Russian national legally resident in the EU, US, or other non-sanctioned country is not "a person located in the Russian Federation" for EO 14071 purposes. No special license required. Payments go to a non-Russian account. SDN screening still required.
Path B · Clean
Require non-Russian legal entity
Designer registers a legal entity in the EU, UAE, UK, or other third country and contracts through it. TF's counterparty is that entity — not a Russia-located individual. Resolves both the OFAC and the payment problem. Standard for cross-border digital platforms working with Russian creatives.
Path C · Workable
Require non-Russian bank account
Russia-resident designer may contract with TF but must receive all payouts into a bank account outside Russia (Georgian, Armenian, or EU account). Sidesteps payment infrastructure. OFAC still requires SDN screening of the individual regardless of account location.
Path D · Fallback
OFAC Specific License
For any scenario that remains ambiguous, a US company can apply to OFAC for a specific license. OFAC grants these for commercially reasonable cases outside the prohibited categories. Process takes weeks to months. Source: OFAC licensing overview.
Mandatory baseline for all paths: SDN screening at onboarding and before every payout. Non-compliance is not a defense, and "we didn't know they were on the list" does not eliminate civil liability.
06Key Risks to Watch
Risk Why it matters Action
"Management consulting" prohibition Defined broadly to include strategic business advice and brand management. Any TF feature providing strategic advisory output to a Russia-located designer could be argued to fall under EO 14071's service prohibition. Get written legal opinion before shipping AI-driven brand insights or launch strategy tools to Russia-located users.
Sanctions change frequently New sector determinations were issued December 2025 and January 2025. The framework is actively evolving. An analysis current today may be outdated in 6 months. Build a periodic review cycle into compliance process. Track OFAC Russia updates at ofac.treasury.gov.
50% ownership rule / nominee structures Russian designers may route through third-country entities controlled by SDN-listed individuals. Name screening alone does not catch this. KYC process must include beneficial ownership disclosure for any legal entity counterparty.
Secondary sanctions OFAC has expanded secondary sanctions targeting non-US financial institutions that materially support Russian sanctions evasion. Not directly applicable to TF but affects which payment processors will cooperate. Non-issue if TF uses compliant Western payment infrastructure. Do not route via Russian payment processors.
