Why Now
Why the opportunity to serve emerging designers and micro-influencers in social commerce did not exist before 2024 — and why it exists today.
The Model · The Gap · The Timing
The TF Model
  • Emerging designer lists a limited pre-order drop — orders collected before production, no upfront inventory risk
  • Micro-influencer (10K–50K followers) co-promotes and earns a % of every sale — co-creator economics, not affiliate commission
  • Buyer tries on the garment via VTO before committing to pre-order
  • Platform earns 7% on GMV; 10% on co-creator and ambassador rewards
What LTK and ShopMy Don't Do
  • Both are affiliate-only: creator links to retail products that already exist, earns commission on clicks
  • Neither supports pre-order, drops, or a revenue split tied to the product
  • Neither serves designers without an existing retail presence
  • Both require proven purchase-driving history to join — a catch-22 for new creators
Why Now
  • No funded platform combines open access + pre-order + co-creator economics — confirmed across 7 platforms reviewed
  • VTO developer API available from Sep 2024 at $0.075/session — any platform can integrate it
  • Proof of model: Whatnot fashion +223% YoY; Rhode Beauty $212M revenue on pre-order drops
  • Window closing: ShopMy raised $147.5M across two rounds in 2025 and is expanding into consumer shopping; Vette (Conde Nast) announced for 2026 launch

How ShopMy Won from LTK — The Relevant Benchmark
ShopMy launched in 2020 with a clear wedge against LTK: lower follower threshold (1,000 vs. 5,000+), analytics showing brands exactly which creator drove each sale, automatic commission matching at the highest available rate, and weekly payouts vs. LTK's monthly cycle. Revenue grew from $4M (2023) → $27M (2024) → $80M (2025). They raised $77.5M in a Series B at a $410M valuation in January 2025, then raised a further $70M at a $1.5B valuation in October 2025, reaching 200,000 creators. The playbook: identify the segment the incumbent excludes, build a better product for that segment, take the market.
ShopMy's model is still affiliate-only. Creators link to retail products that already exist; they earn commission when followers buy. No drops. No pre-order. No co-creator revenue split on shared inventory. No community layer. Sacra notes that aggressive scaling risks eroding ShopMy's brand positioning — they are building toward premium, not toward a fundamentally different model. The segment ShopMy cannot serve: designers without existing retail presence, creators who want to co-own what they sell rather than affiliate-link it, and any commerce model that requires pre-order or inventory-gated drops.
Source: Sacra ShopMy Revenue & Business Model Profile, 2025 — sacra.com/c/shopmy

03Why Now — Timing Factors and the Closing Window
The excluded segment has existed for years. Three things changed in 2023–2025. The window is open; it is not permanently open.
Factor 01
VTO became available to any developer
Before September 2024, there was no commercial API offering per-session garment VTO at under $0.10/image for third-party developers. Snap's AR try-on required direct brand partnership deals; Google's VTO ran inside Google Search only. FASHN.ai launched the first developer API at $0.04/image in September 2024 (updated to $0.075/image, effective March 2025). Any platform can now integrate photorealistic garment try-on with an API key — no enterprise contract, no minimum commitment. Source: FASHN.ai blog, Jan 2025 · fashn.ai/blog/pricing-update-for-developer-api
Factor 02
Fashion drops and pre-order models are proven at scale
Whatnot's women's fashion category grew 223% year-over-year in 2025, with fashion buyers placing 12M+ orders per month on a live-drops format. Monthly buyer retention: 80%+. Rhode Beauty built a 440,000-person waitlist for its first launch using community pre-registration and controlled release — reached $212M in revenue in 12 months and sold to Elf Beauty for $1B in 2025. Both confirm that fashion consumers commit to and return to time-limited drops formats at scale. Sources: Sacra Whatnot profile, 2025 · Whatnot 2026 Trends Report · Optimonk — Rhode marketing breakdown
Factor 03
Gen Z discovers through micro-influencers, not celebrity
22% of Gen Z and Gen Alpha discover new brands via micro-influencers — near parity with celebrity influencers (27%). Gen Z/Alpha are 1.5× more likely to discover brands through social media than older generations and 2× more likely to cite influencer content as a purchase driver. They are also 20 percentage points less likely to buy the same brand consistently, prioritizing unique, emotionally resonant products. Gen Z/Alpha projected to account for 40% of global fashion spending by 2035. The trust trend supports the timing: Gen Z and Millennial trust in influencers grew from 51% to 61% between 2019 and 2023 — a 10-point shift over four years. Sources: BCG + Women's Wear Daily, October 2025 — survey of 9,000+ US consumers · Morning Consult, 2024 — n=2,200 (2019), n=2,204 (2023)

The Window Closes
ShopMy raised $147.5M across two rounds in 2025 — $77.5M Series B at a $410M valuation in January, and a further $70M at a $1.5B valuation in October. Vette — a Conde Nast creator-operated commerce platform with a revenue-share model — announced for early 2026 launch. TikTok Shop has a pre-order feature, currently gated to select sellers with Account Managers; ByteDance product velocity means further development is probable. None of these are TF's product today. They are signals that the model's commercial viability is now visible to well-funded players.
The window is open because the specific combination — open access + pre-order + co-creator economics at scale — has not been built. That gap is not guaranteed to persist to 2027–2028.
