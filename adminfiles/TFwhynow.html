Why Now
Why the opportunity to serve emerging designers and micro-influencers in social commerce did not exist before 2024 — and why it exists today.
Conclusion

LTK requires 5,000+ followers and months of shoppable posting history. ShopMy lowered the technical floor to 1,000 followers — and grew from $4M to $80M in revenue in two years by serving creators LTK rejected. ShopMy is the more relevant competitor, and the more honest benchmark. But ShopMy's model is identical to LTK's at its core: affiliate links, curated storefronts, commission on retail products that already exist. No drops. No pre-orders. No co-creator economics on shared inventory.

ShopMy's own growth story is the playbook. They captured the segment LTK excluded by lowering the entry bar and building better tools. TF's gap is different in kind: designers who have no existing retail product to affiliate-link, creators who want co-ownership of a drop rather than a commission on someone else's inventory, and any commerce model requiring pre-order, community participation, or inventory-gated sales. That gap is not about follower counts. The proof that the model works arrived in 2023–2025: API-accessible VTO at $0.075/session, Whatnot's women's fashion category growing 223% year-over-year with 12M+ monthly orders proving fashion drops drive repeat participation at scale, and Rhode Beauty's 440,000-person waitlist generating $212M in revenue in 12 months showing that community pre-registration and controlled release is a viable commercial model.

ShopMy solved creator access to the existing affiliate model. No one has built the model gap product — yet. ShopMy raised $77.5M in January 2025 and has a stated live-shopping roadmap. Vette (Conde Nast) launched in early 2026 as the first institutional creator-operated commerce platform with a revenue-share model. The window exists because no funded company has built the specific combination of open access + pre-order + co-creator economics at scale. That window is not permanent.

How ShopMy Won from LTK — The Relevant Benchmark
ShopMy launched in 2020 with a clear wedge against LTK: lower follower threshold (1,000 vs. 5,000+), analytics showing brands exactly which creator drove each sale, automatic commission matching at the highest available rate, and weekly payouts vs. LTK's monthly cycle. Revenue grew from $4M (2023) → $27M (2024) → $80M (2025). They raised $77.5M in a Series B at a $1.5B valuation in January 2025 and scaled to 200,000 creators. The playbook: identify the segment the incumbent excludes, build a better product for that segment, take the market.
ShopMy's model is still affiliate-only. Creators link to retail products that already exist; they earn commission when followers buy. No drops. No pre-order. No co-creator revenue split on shared inventory. No community layer. Sacra notes that aggressive scaling risks eroding ShopMy's brand positioning — they are building toward premium, not toward a fundamentally different model. The segment ShopMy cannot serve: designers without existing retail presence, creators who want to co-own what they sell rather than affiliate-link it, and any commerce model that requires pre-order or inventory-gated drops.

03Why Now — Timing Factors and the Closing Window
The excluded segment has existed for years. Three things changed in 2023–2025. The window is open; it is not permanently open.
Factor 01
VTO became available to any developer
Before September 2024, there was no commercial API offering per-session garment VTO at under $0.10/image for third-party developers. Snap's AR try-on required direct brand partnership deals; Google's VTO ran inside Google Search only. FASHN.ai launched the first developer API at $0.04/image in September 2024 (updated to $0.075/image, January 2025). Any platform can now integrate photorealistic garment try-on with an API key — no enterprise contract, no minimum commitment. Source: FASHN.ai blog, Jan 2025 · fashn.ai/blog/pricing-update-for-developer-api
Factor 02
Fashion drops and pre-order models are proven at scale
Whatnot's women's fashion category grew 223% year-over-year in 2025, with fashion buyers placing 12M+ orders per month on a live-drops format. Monthly buyer retention: 80%+. Rhode Beauty built a 440,000-person waitlist for its first launch using community pre-registration and controlled release — reached $212M in revenue in 12 months and sold to Elf Beauty for $1B in 2025. Both confirm that fashion consumers commit to and return to time-limited drops formats at scale. Sources: Sacra Whatnot profile, 2025 · Whatnot 2026 Trends Report · Optimonk — Rhode marketing breakdown
Factor 03
Gen Z discovers through micro-influencers, not celebrity
22% of Gen Z and Gen Alpha discover new brands via micro-influencers — near parity with celebrity influencers (27%). Gen Z/Alpha are 1.5× more likely to discover brands through social media than older generations and 2× more likely to cite influencer content as a purchase driver. They are also 20 percentage points less likely to buy the same brand consistently, prioritizing unique, emotionally resonant products. Gen Z/Alpha projected to account for 40% of global fashion spending by 2035. Source: BCG + Women's Wear Daily, October 2025 — survey of 9,000+ US consumers

The Window Closes
ShopMy raised $77.5M in January 2025 and has a stated live-shopping roadmap. Vette — a Conde Nast creator-operated commerce platform with a revenue-share model — launched in early 2026. TikTok Shop has a pre-order feature, currently gated to select sellers with Account Managers; ByteDance product velocity means further development is probable. None of these are TF's product today. They are signals that the model's commercial viability is now visible to well-funded players.
The window is open because the specific combination — open access + pre-order + co-creator economics at scale — has not been built. That gap is not guaranteed to persist to 2027–2028.
