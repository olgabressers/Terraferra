01Executive Summary — Analysis Findings
01
ShopMy is the threat — not LTK
ShopMy grew 200% in 2025, is profitable at $147.5M raised, and launched a community product (Circles) in July 2025. LTK is mature and flat. If Circles scales, TF's community differentiation weakens. Launching TF is time-sensitive.
02
Pre-production entry + pre-order together are TF's core differentiation
Every affiliate and marketplace platform in this research — LTK, ShopMy, W&B, NOTHS, Ablo — requires existing inventory to participate. TF can sign a designer with a sketch. Because TF runs on pre-order, the designer produces only what is already sold.
03
Co-creation is TF's feature with no competitor equivalent
TF's co-creation layer lets consumers vote on designs before production. No platform in this research has built that. It generates demand data — which designs, colors, sizes get the most votes — before a unit is produced.
04
EU market opportunities
LTK operates in UK and Germany but focuses on established influencers and mass-market brands — not emerging independent designers. ShopMy is US-first with UK expansion just beginning in 2025. No platform in this research has an active recruitment programme targeting EU independent emerging designers for pre-order or co-creation.
05
The fee comparison is not straightforward
TF charges 7% on GMV, but the designer also funds consumer discounts (~30%) and associated costs (~13%) — total designer cost is closer to 50%. The comparison with W&B (~52%) and NOTHS (25%) is not a clear win on price. The argument for designers is the risk model: costs are paid only on confirmed sales, with no inventory produced upfront.
06
Demand data as a B2B revenue opportunity
TF generates pre-production demand data — co-creation votes, pre-order volumes, geographic demand by style — with potential to monetize as B2B trend intelligence for fashion brands and investors, independent of GMV.

03The EU Market Gap
The Gap
Neither W&B nor NOTHS offers co-creation, pre-order, or community revenue sharing.

W&B ($95M GMV, profitable) and NOTHS (~$110M revenue) prove the independent designer market is real in Europe — but both are pure marketplaces. Designer produces, consumer buys. EU consumers buying from US-based platforms (LTK, ShopMy) pay import VAT and customs duties on delivery — EU designer to EU consumer avoids this entirely.

04AI Discovery Tools — Three Sides of the Discovery Problem

Terra Ferra needs in-platform discovery so designers can find the right influencers, the right fans and the right consumers. This catalog covers real, functioning, verified tools across three categories. A — AI APIs Terra Ferra integrates into its own product (the route to making TF itself a discovery platform). B — SaaS Terra Ferra subscribes to from the brand side to find creators. C — tools to find and segment paying consumers and fans. Every card cites the source URLs the facts come from.

A
Visual / AI APIs
Integrate · TF embeds in its product
AI engines Terra Ferra embeds inside its own product. The TF team never opens these tools — they run invisibly on TF's servers. The end user (a designer, a fan, a shopper) gets the benefit. This is the route to making TF itself a discovery platform. Works for both mobile app and web — same Cat A APIs in either.
What it looks like in TF A fan snaps an outfit photo in the TF app (or uploads one on web) → TF surfaces designers whose work matches that look (Syte, FashionCLIP). Designer writes "midi peplum mauve dress" — consumer searches "flowy purple dress" and finds it (Lily AI).
B
Influencer Discovery SaaS
Subscribe · TF logs in to find creators
Paid platforms the TF team uses at the desk to find creators to recruit. Brand side subscribes; creators are surfaced. No engineering required. Monthly cost.
What it looks like for TF A TF account manager logs into Modash, uploads a designer's lookbook, gets a shortlist of influencers whose Instagram aesthetic matches, reaches out, tracks campaign performance. Same workflow on Lefty or Heepsy at different price points.
C
Consumer / Fan Discovery
Integrate & subscribe · find buyers
Tools that find and segment paying customers — not creators. Some embed inside the product (Algolia, Constructor); some sit on the marketing side (Klaviyo, Segment). Predict who will buy, build lookalike audiences, personalise the shopping experience.
What it looks like in TF A returning shopper sees a drop page personalised to their style profile (Constructor). Klaviyo predicts which existing customers will buy next and triggers an email; lookalike audiences sync to Meta and Google ads.

Ideas This Raises for Terra Ferra
01
Day-one stack: TikTok + Instagram + Klaviyo + Heepsy
All four are free or near-free. TikTok and Instagram creator marketplaces are zero-cost native discovery. Klaviyo's free tier covers TF's first 250 profiles per designer. Heepsy at €69/mo is the cheapest paid discovery layer with native lookalike search. Total month-1 spend: ~€69 per designer.
02
FashionCLIP is the integration play, not a vendor licence
FashionCLIP is open-source, MIT-licensed, fine-tuned on 800K fashion products. TF engineering self-hosts and fine-tunes on TF's own catalog — TF owns the visual-discovery IP, no vendor lock-in, zero ongoing licence cost. This is the single highest-leverage Category A option.
03
Lily AI solves the designer / consumer language gap
Designers describe products in fashion-speak ("midi peplum mauve"); consumers search in everyday language ("flowy purple dress"). Lily AI translates between the two at the attribute layer. Direct integration target once TF has catalog volume to justify the licence.
04
Lefty is the upgrade path when TF can fund a fashion-luxury discovery SaaS
Strongest luxury fashion client roster of any influencer-discovery vendor in this scan (Louis Vuitton, Celine, Ralph Lauren, Acne Studios, Lanvin, Byredo). Product seeding + affiliate matches TF's pre-order / ambassador model exactly. Pricing on request — enterprise.
05
Upfluence's customer-to-creator pipeline is TF's own thesis, productised
Upfluence surfaces "creators who already love your brand" from existing customer data. That is the superfan-to-influencer path TF wants to build natively. Integrating Upfluence early gives TF the workflow without building it — and surfaces the requirements TF's own version needs to deliver.
06
Consumer-side: Constructor + Segment from architecture day one
Constructor (API-first AI commerce search) and Segment (CDP backbone) need to be designed into the architecture before TF has volume. Retrofitting either after a year of growth is expensive. Klaviyo on top covers email / SMS / lookalike sync for each designer.
07
Skip the enterprise tier — CreatorIQ, Salesforce, IZEA, Bloomreach
All four are over-scaled for TF and cost $20K+/year minimum. Re-evaluate only after TF crosses $10M GMV or runs 50+ simultaneous influencer campaigns. Before that, every dollar spent here is a dollar not spent on designer / creator acquisition.
08
Flag and re-verify: Vue.ai, Google Vision Product Search, Captiv8

06The Name: Terra Ferra

Why the name works

Distinctive and memorable — two clean words, easy to say in English, Italian, and French. "Terra" signals earthy, artisanal, grounded — the right connotation for independent designers who make things by hand, not mass-market factories. "Ferra" adds sharpness and sounds like a fashion house. The combination is European in feel without being geographically specific.
