Community Growth — Concrete Processes

How each SMTHG. feature drives new members in. Step-by-step entry flows, the drop timeline a community manager runs week by week, and how to diagnose when the flywheel breaks.

01How New Members Arrive — Three Entry Flows

Every new member enters through one of three paths. Knowing which path they came from tells you what to do in the next 72 hours to activate them.

Flow A
Designer Announcement → Community Join
  • 1 Designer posts to their Instagram / TikTok / email list: "My first drop opens Thursday — join my space for 24h early access." Link points to their Brand Space on SMTHG.
  • 2 Follower clicks. Lands on the Brand Space: intro video, drop countdown ("Opens in 2d 14h"), member count visible ("47 people already in").
  • 3 Joins free. Email only — no payment, no app install at this stage. Lands in So-Me-Space with the community feed and other members' styling posts immediately visible.
  • 4 Automated designer welcome message fires within 1 hour. Drop notification queued for T−24h. Member is now in the activation window.
  • 5 Member returns for the drop. Pre-orders, pays deposit. Share & Grow prompt fires immediately after confirmation — peak excitement moment. They invite someone with similar taste.
  • 6 Inside So-Me-Space, the member discovers a second designer through the style tribe they joined. Follows that Brand Space. Becomes part of a second community. Flow A restarts with that designer's audience.
Each designer onboarded brings their existing followers — 2,000 to 50,000 warm contacts — as a single acquisition event.
Flow B
Influencer Content → Community Join
  • 1 AI-matched influencer receives brief + early try-on access 3 days before the drop. Gets: tracking link, suggested post timing, and commission rate (10–20% of sales they drive).
  • 2 Influencer uploads their photo into the Virtual Try-On feature. Gets an AI-styled preview of the drop piece. Posts to Instagram / TikTok with their real face in the piece, link in bio.
  • 3 Followers click through. Land on the drop page with a live deposit counter ("22 reserved") and the same try-on tool. Trying on creates emotional attachment before the purchase decision.
  • 4 Follower pre-orders. Shares their own try-on preview to their network. The second wave of posts reaches people who follow a peer — higher trust than a paid creator.
  • 5 Drop sells out. The sold-out card and the sell-out time become the hook for the next influencer brief: "This closed in 58 hours last time." Proof for the next cycle.
One influencer post creates the highest-velocity acquisition event in the drop window. Commission tracking keeps the creator invested in conversion, not just reach.
Flow C
Style Tribe Discovery → Community Join
  • 1 An existing member curates a public board: "Quiet Luxury Picks" — 12 drops saved with their own styling notes. Board is visible in the Quiet Luxury tribe feed and in search.
  • 2 A stranger with the same aesthetic finds the board through a mutual share, a social search, or a DM from a friend. Clicks in — no account required to browse.
  • 3 Lands in the Quiet Luxury tribe: drop feed, "340 saves" on a piece they like, member styling posts, the style-tag library. Joins free to follow the board and see drops in their aesthetic.
  • 4 Clicks into a Brand Space from the tribe feed. Watches the intro video. Enables early-access notification for the next drop. Enters Flow A from step 4 onwards.
  • 5 Posts their own styling in the tribe. Coins a new style name in the tag library. Their content surfaces to the next stranger who discovers the tribe. Loop C continues without any platform action.
The only flow that runs without platform spend, drops, or influencers. It operates on member-created boards and public curation — permanently.

02The Drop Timeline — What the Community Manager Does, Step by Step

Every drop is an acquisition and activation event. Eight steps from announcement to close. Each step has either an automated trigger or a specific CM action.

T−7 days
Designer + Community Manager
Designer publishes drop announcement in their Brand Space: title, piece previews, close date. CM posts a teaser in the relevant style tribe. Platform sends a push notification to all members already following that Brand Space.
↳ "Coming soon" card appears in So-Me-Space for this designer's community.
T−5 days
Community Manager
Tribe engagement post: "What's your version of this aesthetic?" — a question, not a product post. Measures tribe temperature. High engagement → brief one influencer. Low engagement → run a Super-fan challenge to warm the community before the drop.
↳ Tribe response rate informs how many influencers to brief and whether to add a live session at launch.
T−3 days
Platform (automated) + Influencer
AI-matched influencer receives brief: drop details, early try-on access, unique tracking link, commission rate, suggested post date (T−2). Brief includes current tribe engagement numbers as context.
↳ Creator tracking link activated. Commission measurement starts from first click.
T−2 days
Influencer (external post)
Influencer posts virtual try-on content to Instagram / TikTok. CM checks post performance at 6 hours. Under 2% engagement rate → brief a second creator for a post at T−1. Good performance → log the creator for the next drop brief.
↳ New member surge expected within 12–24h. Platform queues welcome messages + drop notification for all incoming joins.
T−1 day
Platform (automated)
24h early-access notification fires to all community members — push and email. "Your 24h window is open. [Brand] drop closes tomorrow." Live deposit counter activates in So-Me-Space: "38 people have already reserved."
↳ Notification open rate measured. Under 25% → queue a second push at T−4h with a different subject line.
Drop opens
Platform + Designer + Community Manager
Drop goes live. Countdown running. Live deposit counter visible to all visitors arriving from external links — real-time social proof. Super-fan studio opens: members generate AI try-on videos for this drop's pieces and share to their own social. Designer optionally runs a live Brand Space session. CM posts in the tribe: "It's open — 72 hours."
↳ Every new visitor sees real-time demand. Each shared try-on video is an unpaid acquisition post reaching that member's full follower base.
+24h post-deposit
Platform (automated)
Share & Grow prompt fires for every member who has just deposited — at the exact moment of peak excitement. "You're in. Share this drop with a friend — if they reserve too, you both get [incentive]." Each friend who deposits also receives the same prompt 24h later. The referral chain compounds automatically.
↳ Track per drop: % of depositors who share, % of shared links that convert to a join. These two numbers give you the viral coefficient for this drop.
Drop closes
Platform + Community Manager
Drop closes. Sold-out card published. CM posts in tribe: "Gone — [X] pieces claimed in [Y] hours." Platform sends a "Missed it?" push to members who viewed the drop but did not deposit. Waitlist opens for a potential re-run. Sold-out stats become the hook for the next drop's influencer brief.
↳ "Missed it" members are the highest-intent audience for the next drop. They already wanted it — they just needed one more reason.

04How Style Tribes Self-Recruit — The Organic Loop

Style tribes are the only part of the platform that grows without drops, influencers, or paid activity. The loop runs entirely on member curation. Seven steps:

1
A member curates a public board
"Coastal Suede — my picks" — 10 drops saved with the member's own styling notes and commentary. The board is visible in their style tribe's feed and in public search.
Feature: Boards & Saves
2
The board reaches a stranger
Another member shares the board link in a DM. Or a friend of the board owner tags it on Instagram. Or it surfaces in SMTHG. tribe search. A non-member clicks in — no account needed to browse.
Feature: Style Tribes — chat & feed
3
Stranger enters the tribe
Sees the Coastal Suede tribe: drop feed, "380 members," trending boards, member styling posts. Joins free to follow the board and get drop notifications for this aesthetic.
Feature: Style Tribes — chat & feed
4
New member clicks into a Brand Space from the tribe feed
Sees a drop card from a designer whose aesthetic matches. Clicks into their Brand Space. Watches the intro video. Enables early-access notification for the next drop. Enters the drop flow.
Feature: Brand Space — intro video + drop countdown
5
New member coins a style name
While saving a drop, the member types a new style tag: "Coastal Suede." This enters the style-tag library — a discovery vocabulary that belongs to the platform and grows with every contribution. The member earns a point for creating it.
Feature: Style-Tag Library (via Points & Leaderboard)
6
Tag surfaces in cross-tribe discovery
"Coastal Suede" appears in trending tags. Members from adjacent aesthetics (Coastal Minimalist, Quiet Luxury) find it. Content clusters form around the tag. More boards accumulate under it. The tribe feed grows without any CM action.
Feature: Style-Tag Library + Boards & Saves
7
The board's save count increases — attracting the next stranger
"Saved by 340 people" is visible to every visitor. As the count grows, the board becomes more compelling to click into, more prominent in search, and more likely to be shared. Loop restarts at step 2.
Feature: Boards & Saves — public save counts

05Community Manager — Weekly Operations

The minimum weekly rhythm to keep the flywheel spinning across 3–5 active designer communities simultaneously. Every row is a specific action, not a goal.

Day Focus Action What to decide
MON Metrics Review last week: 72h activation rate per community, referral rate per drop, total new members, drop GMV. Compare each number to the previous drop. Flag any community where activation < 30% or referral rate < 20%. These are the two stall signals requiring a fix this week.
TUE Next Drop Brief the next drop's designer: confirm announcement post date, copy draft, and which social channel to prioritise. Send the AI-matched influencer their brief with early try-on access and tracking link. Does the influencer match this specific drop's aesthetic? Capacity this week? If not — find a second match now, not at T−1.
WED Tribes Post a "name this style" prompt in 2 style tribes. Feature the top board from the last 7 days in the tribe feed. Review Super-fan studio content from the previous drop — feature the best 2–3 submissions with creator credit. Is the tribe active between drops, or only during drop windows? If mid-week activity is low — add a live Brand Space session to the upcoming drop schedule.
THU Drop Prep Verify drop countdown is live, quantities are correct, and the early-access notification list is confirmed. If a live Brand Space session is scheduled for launch, send the designer the session brief and confirm the time. Is the drop opening at a time when the target audience is online? Avoid Saturday midnight and Monday 7am for the first open.
FRI Activation Identify everyone who joined Monday–Wednesday but has taken no action yet. Queue a push notification: "A drop you'll love closes Sunday — your early access is waiting." Check influencer post 48h performance. Under 2% engagement → brief a second creator for a Saturday post. Are new joins arriving from influencer posts or from referrals? The source tells you which of the three flows is carrying the most weight this week.
SAT Creators Review Super-fan studio submissions from the current drop. DM the top 3 creators: acknowledge their submission and note if they are being featured. Post the featured content in the tribe feed with their credit tag. Is the studio active? If fewer than 5 submissions — the drop brief did not generate enough excitement. Adjust the brief format for the next drop.
SUN Discovery Surface a second designer to all members who were active in this week's drop community. "You might also like [Brand B]." Preview next week's drop in the tribe feed. Send a weekly wrap to style tribes: what happened, what's coming. Are members following only one Brand Space? Cross-designer discovery is the step that turns a one-drop purchase into a platform habit.

06When the Flywheel Stalls — Three Warning Signals

Each stall maps to a specific broken stage. Diagnose by the signal; fix that stage only.

Signal 1
New members not activating — under 30% take any action in the first 72 hours
What it means Members joined, saw nothing compelling immediately, and forgot the platform exists. The join was easy; the reason to return was absent.
Where the flywheel breaks Stage 3: Drop urgency is not reaching new members fast enough. The gap between join moment and first drop is too long for the activation window to hold.
Fix: Fire the welcome drop notification within 2h of every new join — not only at T−24h. Move the next drop earlier if the calendar allows. Add a designer welcome message that names the specific drop date and the countdown.
Signal 2
Referral rate below 20% — Share & Grow is not converting
What it means The Share & Grow prompt fires at the wrong moment — either before the member has emotionally committed, or after post-purchase excitement has faded.
Where the flywheel breaks Stage 5: The referral chain does not start. K-factor stays below 1.0. Every drop requires a full influencer or designer-driven acquisition effort with no compounding from existing members.
Fix: Move Share & Grow to fire immediately after deposit confirmation. Test two variations: instant (0h delay) vs 2h post-deposit. Add a specific incentive: "Your friend gets 24h early access on their first drop."
Signal 3
Drop pre-orders declining — next drop converting lower than the previous
What it means The community goes cold between drops. Members who were active in drop one have disengaged by drop two. No mid-drop activity means no habit — the platform is behaving like a one-off shop.
Where the flywheel breaks Stage 6: Members are not discovering a second designer. The platform is not becoming a habit — it is a single-brand experience with repeat drops.
Fix: Add a mid-week live Brand Space session between drops (20 min Q&A). Run a Super-fan challenge in the interim week. Surface a second designer to every member who pre-ordered the first drop — cross-designer discovery creates the return habit.
The one number that tells you whether the flywheel is working: the viral coefficient. K = (share rate per depositor) × (join rate per shared link). K above 1.0 means the community grows without paid acquisition. K below 1.0 means it leaks. Track it per drop, not per month.
