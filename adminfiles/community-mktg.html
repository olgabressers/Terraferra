01The Community Flywheel

A flywheel is a self-reinforcing loop. Each stage feeds the next. The critical insight: community members do the marketing. Every purchase generates social proof. Every referral lowers cost of acquisition. Every drop creates urgency that triggers sharing. The platform's job is to design the triggers — not run the campaigns.

02Three Growth Loops — How New People Come In

SMTHG has three distinct loops that bring new members in. Each operates independently — they compound when all three run simultaneously. A new member acquired via any loop can become the seed of all three.

Loop A
Designer-Led Loop
  • 1Designer announces upcoming drop to their existing audience (Instagram, email, TikTok)
  • 2Audience clicks to So-Me-Space — joins free to access the drop calendar and early access
  • 3New members see other designers' communities inside the platform
  • 4They follow a second designer → become part of a second community
  • 5They invite friends with the same taste → friends join and discover both designers
Each designer onboarded brings their audience — typically 2,000–50,000 warm followers — as a single acquisition event.
Loop B
Influencer-Led Loop
  • 1Influencer joins designer's community in So-Me-Space (as a Founding Creator or organic member)
  • 2Influencer posts about the drop — styled try-on content, "I just reserved this" moment, countdown
  • 3Followers click through to SMTHG — join free to access the same drop
  • 4Some followers also pre-order → create social proof for their own followers
  • 5Drop sells out → scarcity proof → next influencer posts drives even faster uptake
Influencer posts are the highest-velocity acquisition event. One post from a 50K fashion creator can add 200–1,000 new members in 48 hours.
Loop C
Member Referral Loop ("Share & Grow")
  • 1Existing member pre-orders a piece and gets a "Share & Grow" prompt
  • 2Member shares their unique link with a friend who has similar taste
  • 3Friend joins So-Me-Space → sees the same drop that was shared
  • 4Friend also pre-orders → becomes a member → gets their own "Share & Grow" prompt
  • 5Referral compounds: each member brings ~1.3–2 more members over time
The referral loop is the only one that runs without any platform action. It operates continuously between drops as long as members stay engaged.

03Community Mechanisms — What Each One Does on SMTHG

Every community marketing mechanism has a specific job. Below: the mechanism, how it works in general, and how it maps to a specific SMTHG feature or process.

  • FOMO & Scarcity
    Acquisition trigger
    Limited availability creates urgency. When people know they can miss out, they share to ensure their friends also get access — expanding the audience as a side effect of the urgency signal.
    SMTHG: Drop countdown timers, limited quantities, 24hr early-access notifications. "48 hrs left" is not just urgency — it triggers sharing ("don't miss this"). The sell-out event becomes proof for the next drop.
  • Social Proof
    Conversion trigger
    People follow other people's purchasing decisions. Seeing "200 members pre-ordered this" or "your friend just reserved X" reduces purchase anxiety and accelerates decisions.
    SMTHG: Shared feed showing activity, pre-order deposit counts visible to community. The "so-me-space" feed is a continuous social proof signal — members see each other acting, which encourages action.
  • Identity & Belonging
    Retention mechanism
    When members identify with a community ("I'm a SMTHG person", "I follow this designer"), leaving becomes a social loss, not just a product cancellation. Identity attachment is the most durable retention mechanism.
    SMTHG: So-Me-Space is named to trigger personal identity ("so-me" = this is me). Members build style profiles, follow specific designers, and build a curated community circle. The platform reflects their taste — leaving means losing that mirror.
  • UGC (User-Generated Content)
    Organic acquisition
    When community members create content about the platform (photos, reviews, try-on videos), they do the platform's marketing for free. UGC has higher trust than brand content and reaches new audiences organically.
    SMTHG: Virtual try-on is the UGC engine. A member uploads their photo, gets an AI-styled preview, and naturally shares it ("look what I'm getting"). Each shared try-on is an unpaid acquisition post reaching the member's full follower base.
  • Network Effects
    Value compounding
    Each new member makes the community more valuable for existing members. More members = more social proof, more peer feedback, more demand confirmation for designers, more reason to join. Value grows non-linearly.
    SMTHG: More members in a designer's So-Me-Space means more pre-order demand → designer produces more confidently → better drops → more members join. The community is the demand signal that makes the designer's product better.
  • Peer Recommendation
    Trust-based acquisition
    A recommendation from a friend converts at 4–10× the rate of a brand advertisement. Peer recommendations are the highest-trust acquisition channel — and the hardest to manufacture. The platform's job is to make sharing frictionless.
    SMTHG: "Share & Grow" is the peer recommendation mechanism. The key is to trigger it at the right moment — right after a pre-order deposit, when excitement is highest. That is the moment members most want to tell someone.
  • Live Events & Sessions
    Depth & loyalty
    Real-time events (Q&As, design sessions, live try-ons) create moments that cannot be replicated asynchronously. Members who attend feel a direct relationship with the creator — dramatically increasing loyalty and repeat purchase rate.
    SMTHG: "Live brand sessions and events" inside So-Me-Space. These are the highest-loyalty moments on the platform. Each live session is also a recruitment opportunity — members share the invite before the event ("come watch this designer's session").
  • Exclusive Access
    Entry barrier & status
    When non-members can see that members get something they don't (early access, exclusive drops, first look), joining becomes desirable. Exclusivity is a pull mechanism — it makes outsiders want in without the platform having to advertise.
    SMTHG: Community members get 24hr early-access to drops before the general public. Non-members who hear about a sold-out drop want to be first next time — which drives community joins ahead of the next drop.

05Activation — Turning Joiners into Buyers

Most people who join a community never buy. Activation is the moment a passive member takes their first meaningful action — a pre-order deposit, a try-on share, attending a live session. The window for activation is short: 72 hours after joining. If a new member sees nothing compelling in the first 3 days, they disengage permanently.

06What to Measure — Community Health Metrics

Community health cannot be measured by follower counts alone. The metrics below tell you whether the flywheel is spinning. If any one of them is stalled, the loop is broken at that stage.

Acquisition
  • New members per dropTarget: 50+ per drop at launch
  • % joining via referral (Loop C)Target: 30%+ of joins
  • Designer audience conversion rate% of designer's followers who join So-Me-Space
  • Influencer post → join rateTrack per post; target 1–3% of views
Activation
  • 72h activation rate% who take action in first 3 days
  • Try-on → deposit conversion% who try on and then pre-order
  • First drop participation rate% of new members who pre-order on first drop
  • Time to first actionTarget: under 48h from join
Retention & Depth
  • Repeat pre-order rate% who buy across 2+ drops
  • Cross-designer discovery% following 2+ designer spaces
  • Live session attendance% of active members attending at least 1/month
  • 30/60/90-day retention% still active at each interval
Flywheel Speed
  • Viral coefficient (K-factor)Each member invites how many? Target: K > 1.0
  • Drop-to-drop growth% increase in community size per drop cycle
  • CAC trendShould decline over time as referrals compound
  • Community GMV share% of total GMV from community members vs new visitors
The one number that tells you if the flywheel is working: the viral coefficient (K-factor). If K > 1.0, each member on average recruits more than one new member — the community grows without paid acquisition. If K < 1.0, the platform is leaking. K = (referrals sent per member) × (conversion rate of those referrals).
