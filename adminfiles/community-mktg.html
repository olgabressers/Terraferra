01MVP Launch Marketing — Zero or Minimum Budget, US Market

Zero budget is workable at MVP stage. The highest-ROI tactics — founder outreach, TikTok organic, waitlist referral, and fashion school partnerships — cost nothing but time. Paid spend only makes sense once there is traffic to retarget. Install the Meta pixel on day 1 (free) so the audience builds silently; activate ads later.

Minimum Budget Add-ons — $500–2K/Month

$200–500 · Product cost only
Creator gifting
Send 3–5 micro-influencers (10K–50K US followers, fashion niche) a free product from pilot designers. Request content in exchange — no cash required. The creator gets product; the designer gets UGC and reach. Cost is the product, not a media fee.
$20–50/day · Test only on proven content
TikTok Spark Ads
Boost organic TikTok posts that already show strong engagement (100K+ views organically). Do not boost cold content. Budget $20–50/day for 5–7 days; measure cost-per-click and stop if above $1.50. Only activate once there are 3–5 high-performing organic posts.
Install now · Spend later
Meta Pixel — install on day 1
Install the Meta Pixel on the SMTHG site from launch day at zero cost. It starts building retargeting audiences silently. By the time there is traffic (500+ visitors/month), the audience pool is already sized. Activating retargeting ads at that point costs $15–30/day and targets warm visitors only.
Sequencing matters: Do not spend on paid media before there is traffic to retarget or organic content that's proven to convert. The correct order is: (1) founder outreach + waitlist, (2) TikTok organic, (3) Meta Pixel install (passive), (4) Spark Ads on proven content, (5) Meta retargeting once 500+ visitors/month.

02Why Community Marketing — Not Just Influencer Commerce

Core thesis: ShopMy is a commission tool — a link in a bio. SMTHG is a community platform — designers build a following that buys together, returns, and recruits. These are different categories with different economics and a different story for investors.

Community marketing means building a space where customers gather around a brand or designer — not just pushing links. Members co-create, recommend, and retain each other. The platform captures value from that community loop, not just from the first transaction.

Dimension ShopMy (commission tool) SMTHG (community platform)
Mechanism Influencer shares link → follower clicks → buys → commission paid Designer builds community → members co-create, pre-order, recruit → repeat purchase
Retention Low — each post is a one-time event High — community membership creates ongoing identity and loyalty
CAC over time Flat or rising — every sale needs a new post Declining — word-of-mouth compounds; 68% of new members join via referral Source: marketingltb.com, 2026
LTV impact Marginal uplift from influencer trust +46% higher customer LTV for brands with active communities Source: marketingltb.com, 2026
Investor story Affiliate network (crowded, commoditised) Community-led commerce platform (category claim, defensible)

04The Paid Digital Layer — Retargeting + Creator Whitelisting

Organic influencer posts alone won't be enough at launch. Retargeting converts people who showed intent but didn't buy. Creator whitelisting (the US equivalent of "white posting") amplifies influencer content as targeted paid ads — higher CTR than brand ads because the creative carries the creator's face and audience trust. Both require a US-experienced agency or contractor.
Tactic 01
Retargeting
What it is: Ads served to users who visited SMTHG or a designer's page but did not purchase. Platforms: Meta (Instagram/Facebook), TikTok.

Why it matters: Most first-time visitors don't buy. Retargeting captures intent already expressed — it's the most efficient paid spend for an early-stage platform.

Cost model: CPM/CPC-based. Budget controlled. Can start small ($500–1K/month per designer) and scale with GMV.
Tactic 02
Creator Whitelisting (US term for "white posting")
What it is: The influencer grants SMTHG or the brand permission to run paid ads through their social handle. The ad appears as an organic post from the creator — not a brand ad — but is targeted and paid.

Why it's effective: Carries the creator's identity and audience trust. Typically 2–5× higher CTR vs standard brand ads on the same platform.

US terminology: Also called "creator licensing", "dark posts via influencer handle", or "paid amplification." Platforms: Meta Business Suite (Instagram/Facebook), TikTok Ads via Spark Ads.

05Optional Paid Digital Marketing as a Brand Service

Direction under evaluation — not a decided offering. The idea: SMTHG manages retargeting and creator whitelisting for brands as an optional add-on, charging a management fee or markup on ad spend. This needs economic modelling before any commitment.

If SMTHG runs paid digital for brands, it moves from a pure platform (GMV commission) to a managed services layer. The economics are different and the operational requirements are higher.

Model option How it works Risk
% of ad spend SMTHG charges brands 10–20% of managed ad budget as a management fee. Brand retains budget control. Low margins unless ad budgets are large ($5K+/month per brand)
Flat monthly package $X/month covers setup, pixel, retargeting + whitelisting management. Optional add-on to platform subscription. SMTHG carries delivery risk; requires in-house or dedicated contractor capacity
Performance share SMTHG takes additional GMV commission on sales attributed to paid ads run by SMTHG. Attribution is messy; disputed by brands at payout time
Recommendation before building this: Confirm whether the founding designer pilots want paid media management — or just the platform tools. If they don't, this is premature. Start by offering it informally to 1 pilot and test demand before building a service package.
