02What the Deck Concept Does Not Cover
The deck names viral growth but defines no mechanics, and contains no community layer. Slide 5 states "every share pulls in new consumers, influencers and brands" and slide 3 lists "built to share" outcomes — but no concept element specifies how a share happens, what a consumer gets for sharing, or how users interact with each other outside a purchase. The six dashed elements on the map fill that gap; each links to the internal research page it comes from.
Viral gap
Suggested fill
The deck's loop (slide 5) depends on shares, but sharing is asserted, not designed. The scarcity, identity and status mechanics give each share a concrete trigger: a stock counter to beat, a countdown to act on, a badge or owner card to show. Full detail: Referral & Viral Mechanics, §01–04.
Community gap
Suggested fill
In the deck, consumers relate only to drops — there is no element where users relate to each other. The Super Fan loop (themed content per drop, style-name library) and creator points & tiers create member-to-member activity between drops. Full detail: Gamification research, §01.
Cold-start gap
Suggested fill
Every deck element assumes the consumer arrives via an influencer. Cold discovery — a browsable drops feed, style tags, save-to-board, trending sort — serves consumers with no prior connection, at MVP with manual curation and no ML. Full detail: Referral & Viral Mechanics, §04.
