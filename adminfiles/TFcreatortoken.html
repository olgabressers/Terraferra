TF Business Model & GTM · Research
Creator Payments & Tokenization
Lessons from web3 attempts to tokenize creator revenue through fans. Apps and add-ons enabling fan-to-creator tipping on and around Instagram.
Conclusion · Web3 creator revenue tokenization
Every startup that tried to let fans buy tokens tied to a creator's income either collapsed or got shut down by regulators. Fans treated the tokens like stocks — they bought to profit, not to support the creator.
Most generalist platforms shut down 2023–2025. Survivors are vertical-specific.
  • Rally, Roll, Friend.tech, Mirror.xyz — all closed. BitClout's founder arrested on fraud charges. Mirror.xyz closed September 2025 — this is not a 2022 crypto-winter story.
  • Two survivors, both vertical-specific. Audius (music streaming, 5M+ MAU) survives because it is a real streaming service — tokenization is layered on top. Chiliz/Socios (sports fan tokens, 2.5M users) survives because it is attached to existing football club IP.
  • Fans treated tokens as speculation, not loyalty. When prices fell, communities collapsed. No generalist platform converted token holders into durable communities.
  • US legal risk remains active. SEC pursued creator coins as unregistered securities. Direct risk for a US Delaware C-corp. Note: SEC enforcement posture shifted post-2024 — but the speculative token model itself is what failed, independent of regulation.
  • No generalist model has worked at emerging-creator scale. Every platform required celebrity-scale audiences or collapsed before mainstream adoption.
Conclusion · Instagram tipping — apps & add-ons
There is no app, plugin, or browser extension that adds a tip button inside Instagram. Every tipping tool (Ko-fi, Patreon, etc.) requires the fan to leave Instagram and go to a separate page — which most fans don't do.
No third-party tool is inside Instagram — by design
  • Ko-fi, Buy Me a Coffee, Patreon, Linktree — all link-in-bio only. Fans must leave the app to tip.
  • No browser extension for Instagram tipping exists in mainstream active use.
  • This is Instagram's deliberate policy, not a market gap. Meta's API does not allow third parties to transact inside the app. Meta built the in-app mechanics itself (Gifts, Badges) and controls the infrastructure. No third party will fill this gap.
  • Instagram Gifts (Reels, 500-follower minimum) is the lowest-friction in-app option. $0.01/Star to creator. Payout threshold: $25.
  • TikTok LIVE gifting is a closer precedent for TF's use case — fans send virtual gifts during live drops that convert to real income, alongside creator commissions. See Section 05.
Relevance for Terra Ferra · Research — Not Decided
Web3 tokenization as a TF business model Not recommended for v1

Most generalist platforms failed within 3–5 years. The pattern is consistent: fans buy tokens as speculation, not community membership. When prices fall, the community collapses. Vertical-specific survivors (Audius, Chiliz) work because they have real underlying utility and existing large fanbases — neither condition applies to TF at pre-revenue stage.

TF is a US Delaware C-corp. The SEC has treated creator coins as unregistered securities. Adding a token layer before product-market fit compounds legal risk on top of execution risk.

The relevant insight from this space: fans want access and status, not financial exposure to creators. This is already the direction for TF's mechanics — limited-edition drops, creator recognition, early-backer access — without requiring tokens or securities risk.

Post-2024 note: SEC enforcement posture shifted after 2024. Non-speculative on-chain loyalty credentials (not tradeable tokens) are an emerging model worth monitoring at v2+ stage — not for v1.

Fan-to-creator tipping as a TF feature Possible · Low priority

A tipping layer is technically simple — a "support this creator" button tied to a standard payment processor. No blockchain needed. No securities risk.

TF's commission model (10% ambassador, 10% co-creator) routes income to creators through completed transactions. Commission income is tied to actual product demand; tips depend on fan discretion in the moment. The two are complementary, not competing — TikTok's live commerce model runs both in parallel.

The most relevant TF use case for tipping: the period when a creator is onboarding but has not run a drop yet. A tip button gives fans a way to support a creator before there is a product to buy — a creator acquisition signal, not a primary revenue line.

If added, the natural form is post-drop: fans who received their order can optionally tip the creator.

Not a v1 priority. Post-drop tipping is the most natural entry point if added later.

02 What This Means for Each Participant Research · Not Decided
Consumers
  • Tipping gives fans a way to support a creator without making a purchase — a signal of loyalty outside of transactions.
  • Web3 tokens give financial exposure to a creator's success. All evidence shows fans don't want this — they want access and recognition, not investment risk.
  • Instagram Gifts (Reels) is the lowest-friction option today: one tap, inside the app, available in 60+ countries.
  • TF's pre-order mechanic already channels consumer intent into a purchase — a stronger action than a tip.
Influencers & Creators
  • Tipping = additional income on top of commission, not conditional on a completed drop.
  • Instagram Gifts already available at 500+ followers; Badges at 10K+. Creators can use these today, independent of TF.
  • Web3 creator coins promised brand-value upside. In practice, every creator who tried this saw their community collapse when token prices fell.
  • TF's 10% commission routes income through completed transactions — more predictable and higher per-interaction than discretionary tips.
TF Platform
  • A tip feature generates a platform cut on each transaction — rate not set, v2+ consideration only.
  • Commission on completed drops produces a demand signal (what sold, what didn't) that tips do not. The two are complementary revenue streams, not competing ones.
  • No web3 infrastructure needed — a tip button is a standard payment processor integration, not a token or blockchain layer.
  • Not a v1 priority. Post-drop tipping is the most natural entry point if added later.
Designers
  • Tips from fans flow to the creator who promoted the work — not directly to the designer. Designers benefit indirectly through increased drop success.
  • Web3 tokenization of a designer's brand (fans holding "designer coins") has no proven model at this scale. Every attempt collapsed when token prices fell.
  • Pre-order validation is stronger than token speculation as a signal of real demand: fans commit money to a specific product, not a speculative asset.
  • No action required for designers in v1. Relevant only if TF introduces a direct designer-fan support feature post-launch.
03 Web3 Creator Revenue Tokenization — Platform Record
Rally.io
Shut down Jan 2023
Creators launched personal "creator coins" on an Ethereum sidechain. Fans bought coins tied to a specific creator's brand and community. Raised $57M in 2021 (a16z, Coinbase Ventures, Battery Ventures, Canaan).
Token fell ~96% from April 2021 peak of $1.38 to ~$0.01 by October 2022. When the sidechain closed, user assets were stranded and non-transferable.
CEO cited: macro headwinds, expensive legacy sidechain infrastructure, and loss of third-party support. Infrastructure cost was not covered by platform revenue.
Source: Decrypt, Jan 2023 · CoinDesk, Jan 2023
Roll (tryroll.com)
App closed May 2025
Ethereum-based social token infrastructure. Creators minted personal tokens; fans earned or held them as community access passes. Launched 2019–2020.
March 2021: attacker compromised Roll's hot wallet private keys, stole ~$5.7M in social tokens across 42 creator pools (WHALE, FWB, KARMA, JULIEN, etc.), dumped them on Uniswap. Roll created a $750K creator relief fund. Custodial app closed May 26, 2025; protocol components (staking, minting) remain operational on-chain.
Custodial hot wallets holding creator community assets are a single point of failure. One breach destroys trust across the entire ecosystem simultaneously.
Source: Decrypt, Mar 2021 · Roll blog, May 2025
BitClout / DeSo
Founder arrested Jul 2024
Own blockchain. Every user — including celebrities whose profiles were scraped from Twitter without consent — had a "creator coin" whose price rose with the number of holders. Fans speculated on people's future reputation.
Raised ~$257M in token sales (disclosed 2024). Founder Nader Al-Naji (pseudonym "Diamondhands") arrested by FBI July 2024, charged with wire fraud and unregistered securities offerings. Allegations included spending $7M+ on personal expenses including Beverly Hills mansion rent and cash gifts. DOJ dropped criminal case February 2025; SEC dropped civil case March 2026. DeSo blockchain technically still exists.
Tokenizing human identity creates speculative markets in people's reputations, attracts securities regulators, and produced the most serious legal outcome of any platform in this category.
Source: Fortune, Jul 2024 · CoinDesk, Jul 2024
Friend.tech
Shut down Sep 2024
Blockchain app on Base (Ethereum L2). Users bought "keys" (previously "shares") in specific creators — paying ETH for access to private chat rooms with that creator. Launched August 2023; no disclosed external funding.
Peak: $52M deposits, $2M/day in fees (Oct 2023). By mid-2024: deposits down 92% to $4M, daily fees under $100. FRIEND token fell 98% from launch price. The team received ~$44M of the ~$90M total protocol fees (the protocol design split fees 50/50 between team and creators from the start), then transferred contract ownership to a null address.
Access-key mechanics produce speculative buying spikes with no durable utility. When novelty fades there is nothing to hold users. Creators have no incentive to stay once their key price declines.
Source: DL News, Sep 2024
Mirror.xyz
Shut down Sep 2025
Ethereum-based writing and publishing platform. Writers minted articles as NFTs, crowdfunded writing projects via token sales, split revenue on-chain with collaborators. Founded by Denis Nazarov (former a16z crypto partner); backed by a16z, Union Square Ventures, and Atelier Ventures.
Acquired by Paragraph in May 2024; team pivoted to building a separate social app ("Kiosk"). Mirror shut down September 2025; all content automatically redirected to Paragraph.
NFT minting of articles had no clear value proposition for readers once the crypto bull market cooled. Writing and publishing is not a natural fit for financial speculation.
Source: CoinDesk, May 2024
Audius
Active (rebranded Jan 2026)
Decentralized music streaming platform. Artists upload directly; earn AUDIO tokens; fans stake AUDIO to support artists and earn governance rights. No per-stream royalties to intermediaries. Funding: $5.5M Series A led by General Catalyst (2018); separate $3.1M strategic round led by Multicoin Capital (2020). Notable artists: deadmau5, Skrillex.
5M+ monthly active users. July 2022: attacker exploited a governance contract initialization bug, transferred 18.5M AUDIO (~$6M) from community treasury, swapped to ~704 ETH on Uniswap. 2025: Artist Coin Launchpad launched (October 2025), letting verified artists mint their own tokens. Infrastructure relaunched as "Open Audio Protocol" January 2026.
The most durable survivor of this cohort — but AUDIO token price remains far below peak, and per-artist pay cannot compete with Spotify/Apple at scale.
Source: Bleeping Computer, Jul 2022 · audius.co
Chiliz / Socios.com
Active (sports only)
Blockchain infrastructure for sports fan tokens. Football clubs (PSG, Barcelona, Juventus, Arsenal, Man City, etc.) issue branded tokens giving holders voting rights on minor club decisions and access to rewards. Founded 2018; Socios.com product from 2019.
75 brands across 23 countries. ~2.5M Socios.com users. UK Advertising Standards Authority banned Socios/Arsenal ads for misleading fans about investment risk. PSG token fell 34% one week after launch and 73% four months after (Messi signing hype faded). UK MPs stated clubs were "extracting money from loyal supporters with promises that fail to materialise."
The only surviving large-scale model in this space is attached to existing IP with enormous existing fanbases (major football clubs), not built on tokenization alone. Actual utility is thin; tokens trade primarily on speculation around club news events.
Source: Chiliz, 2025 · TechHQ, Oct 2023
04 Key Failure Patterns
1
Tokens as the product, not infrastructure
Platforms launched tokens before building sustainable utility. Token price appreciation drove early adoption; when prices fell, users left. Fan speculation is not fan loyalty.
2
No durable creator incentive
On Friend.tech and Rally, creators had no reason to keep producing once their token price declined. The value of the platform depended on the creator's continued attention, which was not economically guaranteed.
3
Smart contract and custody vulnerabilities
Roll lost $5.7M to a private key compromise (2021). Audius lost $6M to a governance contract exploit (2022). Both happened within the first two years of operation.
4
Securities law risk
The SEC pursued BitClout/DeSo for selling unregistered securities. Friend.tech's structure (buying "shares" in people) attracted regulatory scrutiny. The 2023–2024 SEC enforcement wave made token-based creator platforms legally hazardous in the US.
5
Infrastructure cost vs. revenue
Rally explicitly cited infrastructure costs as a shutdown reason. Proprietary sidechain maintenance was not covered by transaction fees or platform revenue at scale.
6
Token launches as founder exit events
Friend.tech's team extracted ~$44M in fees then abandoned the platform. BitClout's founder is alleged to have spent user funds on personal expenses. Platform structure allowed founders to monetize early without accountability.
7
Speculation fades without utility
Every platform that relied on token price appreciation as the primary value proposition collapsed when market conditions turned. The one survivor with meaningful user numbers (Audius) is built around a real service — music streaming — with tokenization layered on top, not as the core proposition.
05 Instagram Native Tipping & Monetization Features
Key finding: Instagram's in-app native features (Gifts, Badges, Subscriptions) are the only frictionless fan payment path inside Instagram. Meta's API deliberately blocks third parties from transacting inside the app — this is policy, not a market gap. All third-party tools require the fan to leave Instagram via bio link.

TikTok precedent: TikTok LIVE Gifts let fans send virtual gifts (converted to real income) during live streams — alongside TikTok Shop's creator commission model. This is the closest live precedent for TF's hybrid model (tips + commission on drops). TF's emerging-designer creators are often more active on TikTok than Instagram in the sub-$1M revenue tier.
