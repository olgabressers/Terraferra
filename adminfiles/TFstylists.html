Stylist Layer — Existing Platforms & AI Wardrobe Building Tooling

Research on the two adjacent markets relevant to adding stylists to Terra Ferra: (1) platforms connecting stylists with end consumers, and (2) AI tools that help stylists build and manage wardrobes for clients. Every entry below is sourced; entries that could not be verified are listed separately at the bottom of each section. Accessed 2026-05-31.
Conclusion
Stylists are not required as a 6th participant role in v1. Map them to the existing co-creator commission line (10%) as a stylist credential, and defer the dedicated-role decision until after USD 10K MRR and the post-bridge raise.
01 What This Means for the Stylist Layer in Terra Ferra Research, not decided

Gap in the Researched Set

The stylist-to-consumer space is consolidating around either retailer-owned platforms (Stitch Fix, Nordstrom in-house personal styling, Lookiero+Outfittery) or chat-luxury (Threads Styling). Among the verified entries, no live platform connects independent stylists to independent premium designers + their community in one place.

AI Tooling for Stylists

Most verified AI styling tools are built for enterprise retailers (Stylyze, Stylitics, Intelistyle, Vue.ai, YesPlz). Consumer wardrobe apps (Whering, Acloset, Combyne, Cladwell) have no stylist-pro tier. Indyx is the only verified hybrid with a built-in stylist marketplace + virtual closet — its closet-cataloging + 1:1 Lookbook model is the closest reference for a TF stylist tier.

Possible Slot in TF Ecosystem

Stylists map most cleanly to the existing co-creator revenue line (10%) — they curate outfits / edits / lookbooks from designer products and earn commission when those convert. Avoids adding a separate service-marketplace workflow.

Open Questions Before Adding

(1) How does the stylist role differ functionally from the influencer role on TF? (2) Do stylists bring client-book conversion (HNW clients, celebrity dressing rooms) that justifies a 6th participant role? (3) Would TF use a third-party AI styling backend (Stylitics, Intelistyle, YesPlz) or build native — and at what stage?

02 Industry Context Market size · AI adoption · Consolidation
Market Size · Online & Virtual Personal Styling

No audited primary data available in this category.

The category is tracked only by secondary-research firms (Zion Market Research, Future Data Stats, Intel Market Research) whose methodology and sample base are not independently verifiable. Estimates from these firms vary widely — from a few-billion-USD niche to a trillion-USD super-category — depending on how "personal styling" is bundled with adjacent retail. None of these figures should anchor a TAM thesis.

The category does not appear in audited IBISWorld or Statista deliverables that are publicly accessible. Primary investor-grade data is unavailable as of 2026-05-31. Conclusions on this page rely on competitive-landscape observation, not on a sized market.

AI Adoption in Fashion

Industry-wide AI adoption is well-documented at executive level. No primary survey of AI adoption among individual personal stylists could be located.

From the BoF–McKinsey State of Fashion 2025 / 2026 reports:

  • 75% of fashion executives prioritise AI for demand forecasting, inventory and cost control
  • 50% prioritise AI-driven discovery (search, recommendations, virtual assistants)
  • 45% see AI-driven marketing as a major value driver

Sources: BoF State of Fashion 2026 · McKinsey State of Fashion 2025 (PDF)

Direction · 2022–2025

The stylist-marketplace segment is consolidating. Independent startups are being absorbed or shutting down; survivors merge for scale.

  • Trunk Club shut down May 2022 after Nordstrom USD 197M write-down · BoF
  • The Yes acquired by Pinterest, app shut down post-acquisition · FashionNetwork
  • Threads Styling acquired by Chalhoub Group (Dubai) Sept 2022 · FashionUnited
  • Stylyze acquired by Neiman Marcus Group 2021–22 · BoF
  • Le Tote shut down spring 2024 after parent loan default · Wikipedia
  • Lookiero + Outfittery merged March 2025 — pan-European stylist-marketplace consolidation · FashionUnited
  • Mad Street Den (Vue.ai parent) acquired by M2P, March 2025 · vue.ai
03 AI Tools for Stylists & Wardrobe Building 3 vendor candidates · 1 competitive reference

Three enterprise-B2B vendors are usable as a styling backend that TF could integrate (API / white-label). All three serve outfit assembly + style search from a product catalog — they do not cover virtual try-on or AI video / photo generation (those are separately tracked elsewhere). Indyx is included as the closest competitive reference for a hybrid stylist-marketplace, not as a vendor.

Stylitics
New York, USA
Live Enterprise
AI styling and outfitting platform for retailers and brands. Provides AI-styling, digital merchandising and product-recommendation tools used by retail merchandisers and styling teams.
PricingNot publicly listed (enterprise).
Sources stylitics.com — platform AI Styling product page
Intelistyle
London, UK · founded 2017
Live Enterprise · API
AI fashion stylist platform for retailers. APIs and white-label apps so brands can add personalised recommendations, digital styling and look-building to their own sites and apps.
Notable clientsDolce & Gabbana, MaxMara, Tommy Hilfiger, YOOX Net-a-Porter.
Sources intelistyle.com Metavallon VC — seed Econsultancy
YesPlz AI
US-incorporated · Korean founders
Live Enterprise
Fashion product discovery and AI Stylist for retailers. Includes ChatGPT-powered AI stylist that assembles outfits and matches occasions; virtual mannequin filter; deep product tagging.
Sources yesplz.ai GPT Fashion Stylist feature
Indyx
USA · founded 2021–2022
Reference · Not a vendor Hybrid · Stylist tier
Digital wardrobe + outfit app with a built-in stylist marketplace (Lookbook service) and professional cataloging service. Includes a 1:1 styling tier — closest verified example of a hybrid stylist-marketplace with virtual closet.
Pricing (per myindyx.com/how-it-works)Basic consultation USD 25 · ongoing styling from USD 15/mo · 1:1 styling session USD 110 · professional cataloging USD 295 for 100 items + USD 2/extra item. Additional service tiers may exist — consult the live site.
Sources myindyx.com How it works The Elise Edit — review
Investigated, not a fit for TF (research log):
  • Stylyze — captive to Neiman Marcus since 2022 acquisition; not a vendor.
  • Vue.ai — acquired by M2P (fintech) March 2025; fashion strategic direction uncertain.
  • Stylumia — wrong product (trend forecasting / demand sensing, not styling).
  • Save Your Wardrobe — wrong product (B2B aftercare: repairs, alterations).
  • Whering, Acloset, Combyne, Cladwell — B2C consumer apps; no documented enterprise API or licensing tier.
Unverifiable / out of scope: Mode.ai (likely defunct), LookAR, "The Fold" stylist app, Stylesage, StyleScript, Stitch Fix "Stylist Workbench" (the name is industry shorthand; documented internal AI tool is "Vision", not "Workbench").
04 Platforms Connecting Stylists to Consumers 10 verified entries
Wishi
New York, USA · founded 2015
Live B2C + B2B
Online personal-styling marketplace pairing consumers with vetted human stylists who deliver shoppable style boards and chat.
ModelPer-session styling fee paid by consumer; stylist earns a % of that fee. Also runs Wishi for Brands and Amazon integration on the B2B side.
Pricing (consumer)Tiered per-session styling fees. Current published tier prices vary — consult wishi.me/pricing directly. Stylist rates by tier (per secondary review): USD 50–100 (Upcoming) / 100–400 (Experienced) / 400+ (Exclusive).
Sources wishi.me/pricing The Quality Edit — Wishi review Yahoo Finance — Wishi × Amazon
Stitch Fix
San Francisco, USA · founded 2011
Live B2C
Subscription-style "Fix" boxes curated by ~1,600 employed human stylists supported by internal AI/ML tools (notably "Vision" for style visualisation).
ModelUSD 20 styling fee per Fix, credited toward kept items.
PricingUSD 20 styling fee per Fix (per current stitchfix.com/pricing). Stylist roster reported at ~1,600 employed stylists.
Sources stitchfix.com/pricing US Chamber — AI at Stitch Fix Stitch Fix blog — Vision tool Wikipedia
Trunk Club
Chicago, USA · 2009–2022
Closed B2C
Personal-styling subscription box (originally menswear, later women) powered by employed stylists working from physical "clubhouses."
OutcomeAcquired by Nordstrom for USD 350M in 2014. Shut down May 31, 2022 after USD 197M write-down; customers redirected to Nordstrom's in-house personal-styling service.
Sources BoF — Nordstrom shutters Trunk Club Retail Dive analysis Wikipedia
The Yes
Los Altos, USA · 2018–2022
Acquired · Shut B2C
AI-powered shopping app that learned individual taste graphs to recommend fashion across brands.
OutcomeAcquired by Pinterest in 2022; app and website shut down post-acquisition. Team rolled into Pinterest shopping.
Sources FashionNetwork — Pinterest acquires The Yes
Threads Styling
London, UK · founded 2010
Live B2C + B2B
Chat-based luxury personal-shopping service via WhatsApp / WeChat / Instagram DM; later added e-commerce site and live shopping. Targets HNW Gen-Z / millennial luxury buyers.
ModelNo inventory. Commission paid by 600+ luxury brand/retail partners (Hermès, Chanel, etc.). ~50% of sales via DM.
Consumer pricingNone — commission revenue model.
B2B layer"Threads Connect" launched 2021 gives independent personal shoppers and stylists worldwide access to the platform and sourcing network. Acquired by Chalhoub Group (Dubai) in Sept 2022.
Sources Highland Europe analysis The Industry Fashion FashionUnited — Chalhoub acquisition PRNewswire — Threads Connect launch
Glamhive
Los Angeles area, USA
Live Marketplace
Marketplace connecting consumers with professional and celebrity stylists for consultations, closet detox and makeovers. Separate "Professional" membership for stylists.
ModelMarketplace — stylists set their own rates; Glamhive takes a cut. Stylist membership for service providers.
Stylist hourly range (per glamhive.com)USD 75–450+. Consumer session pricing varies by stylist tier — consult the live listing for current rates.

Wantable
Milwaukee, USA

Try-at-home personal-styling "Edit" service with employed expert stylists.

PricingUSD 20 styling fee per Edit · items typically USD 50–100 · 7 items per Edit.

Designer-clothing rental subscription. Previously offered in-store human personal stylists at 5 physical locations (all closed Aug 2020). Service is now digital-only with no human-stylist personalisation layer.

Lookiero + Outfittery
Bilbao, Spain · Berlin, Germany · merged March 2025
Live · Merged B2C
European online personal-shopping subscription. Customer fills profile, stylist sends curated box, customer keeps what they want. Lookiero founded 2016; merged with Outfittery (Berlin) in March 2025 to form a pan-European stylist-marketplace group.
Combined scaleEUR 130M revenue (2024 pro-forma) · 3M+ active customers · 13 markets. Both brands retained post-merger.
PricingLookiero UK: GBP 10 styling fee per box, refunded against kept items.
Sources FashionUnited — merger announcement lookiero.co.uk outfittery.com
