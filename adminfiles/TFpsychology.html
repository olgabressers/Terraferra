Business Model · Research

Psychology + Fashion — Existing Platforms

Research into platforms combining personality psychology with fashion styling and shopping. Covers live products as of 2026-06-27. Entries sourced from primary URLs; user metrics and funding figures from named press sources.
Conclusion

Photo analysis + personality quiz + designer shopping is not fully combined in one product. PSYKHE (Big 5 psychology → multi-retailer marketplace) is the closest. No platform uses DISC typology for fashion. The three-layer flow — appearance → psychotype → context → outfit — does not exist as a live product.

DISC + Fashion
Not found in any live product as of 2026-06-27
Gap
DISC typology (Dominance / Influence / Steadiness / Conscientiousness) is widely used in HR and business coaching. No live fashion platform applies DISC as a styling input. The model maps onto behaviour and communication style, not aesthetics — the translation to wardrobe choices has not been built.
Closest live useBusiness and executive coaching apps (not fashion)
OpportunityDISC could map onto formality preference, statement vs. understated dressing, risk tolerance in outfit choice
02 What Does Not Exist

Three-Layer Flow — Not Built

Photo → Psychotype → Context → Designer

No platform chains all four: (1) AI photo read of appearance, (2) clinical psychology typology quiz, (3) context layer (occasion / role / impression goal), (4) final outfits drawn from a specific designer marketplace. Each layer exists somewhere in isolation; the chain does not.

Designer-only output

Style DNA and misi route users to mass-market retailers. PSYKHE links to luxury multi-brand retailers, not emerging designers. No platform routes the psychology output specifically into emerging affordable-luxury designer inventory.

03 The Untested Flow Direction, not a decided feature
Step 1
Photo read
AI analyses selfie → appearance profile (coloring, proportions). Already proven at scale by Style DNA and misi.
Step 2
Psychotype quiz
Short DISC-based or Big 5 quiz → temperament profile. Adds a dimension no photo alone can capture.
Step 3
Context layer
Occasion (work / event / everyday), role, intended impression. Narrows from style profile to specific outfit direction.
Step 4
Designer output
Final look shown using TF platform inventory — emerging affordable-luxury designers only. Purchase path built in.
