Marketplace Platform Options

Two platforms proposed to power TF's marketplace backend (2022–2023 RFPs). Current features and pricing sourced from vendor websites, June 2026.

01Proposed Platforms: Marketplacer vs Mirakl

Stack proposed by ChangeCX (now Icreon): commercetools (headless commerce backend) + Marketplacer (marketplace layer) + ChangeCX as system integrator. Proposal sent Sep 28, 2022.
Mirakl RFP: TF sent a requirements document to Mirakl — no pricing response was received.
Note on current pricing: 2022 figures below. Both providers wanted ~1.5% of GMV on top of fixed annual fees. Prices may be lower now with a simpler RFP scope; verify directly before re-engaging. (Verified internally, June 2026)

TF verdict High cost for current stage — triple-stack (commercetools + Marketplacer + SI) is API-first and fully customizable. Drop mechanics and basic commission splits are buildable on top. The issue is cost and complexity relative to TF's current scale, not capability. The social layer (feeds, co-creation, community) still requires full custom development on top of any platform. Wrong scale, not wrong category — platform is technically capable and API-accessible. Built for $10M+ GMV enterprise retailers; pricing and contract structure are not suited for an early-stage startup. The social and co-creation layer still requires custom development regardless.
On customizability: Both platforms above are API-first. Standard e-commerce objects — products, orders, inventory, commissions — are configurable via custom attributes and webhook integrations. A drop is a product with a release date and quantity cap; this is implementable on any platform as a configuration layer. The commerce rail (catalog, checkout, payouts) is covered by both platforms and does not require a rebuild.

What requires genuine custom engineering on top of any marketplace platform: (1) the social layer — content feeds, co-creation projects, community interaction; (2) multi-tier revenue routing — a single transaction splitting simultaneously across seller, ambassador, and co-creator. These are the actual custom-development items, not drops or pre-orders.
