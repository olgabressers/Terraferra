Marketplace Platform Options

Two platforms proposed to power TF's marketplace backend (2022–2023 RFPs). Current features and pricing sourced from vendor websites, June 2026.

01Proposed Platforms: Marketplacer vs Mirakl

Stack proposed by ChangeCX (now Icreon): commercetools (headless commerce backend) + Marketplacer (marketplace layer) + ChangeCX as system integrator. Proposal sent Sep 28, 2022.
Mirakl RFP: TF sent a requirements document to Mirakl — no pricing response was received.
Note on current pricing: 2022 figures below. Both providers wanted ~1.5% of GMV on top of fixed annual fees. Prices may be lower now with a simpler RFP scope; verify directly before re-engaging. (Verified internally, June 2026)

TF verdict High cost for current stage — triple-stack (commercetools + Marketplacer + SI) is API-accessible with a configurable schema. Drop mechanics and basic commission splits are buildable on top. Deep customisation (custom order lifecycle states, non-standard checkout flows) requires Marketplacer's professional services team. The issue is cost and complexity relative to TF's current scale. The social layer still requires full custom development regardless. Wrong scale — actively screens out early-stage — Mirakl's sales process qualifies operators by GMV; pre-revenue companies routinely receive no response. TF's 2022 RFP with no reply is consistent with this. Even if onboarded, pricing and contract structure are not suited for a startup. Social and co-creation layer still requires custom development regardless.
On customizability: Both platforms above are API-first. Standard e-commerce objects — products, orders, inventory, commissions — are configurable via custom attributes and webhook integrations. A drop is a product with a release date and quantity cap; this is implementable on any platform as a configuration layer. The commerce rail (catalog, checkout, payouts) is covered by both platforms and does not require a rebuild.

What requires genuine custom engineering on top of any marketplace platform: (1) the social layer — content feeds, co-creation projects, community interaction; (2) multi-tier revenue routing — a single transaction splitting simultaneously across seller, ambassador, and co-creator; (3) payout compliance — US 1099-K and EU VAT handling per payee across multiple parties in one transaction. Drop mechanics (catalog attributes + basic inventory reservation) are not in this list — those are standard platform configuration.
