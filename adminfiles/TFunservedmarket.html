Unserved Market Segments by ShopMy and LTK
TF Entry Points — Gap with ShopMy and LTK
Gap 01 — Pre-order and drops
LTK only
LTK requires in-stock products by policy. Pre-order and drop designers are excluded by rule.
Drop mechanics are TF's core product.
Gap 02 — Upfront fees
LTKShopMy
LTK: $5K–$35K/year. ShopMy: ~$999/month. Both billed before any sales — unaffordable for designers under $300K–$500K revenue.
Price entry lower than LTK and ShopMy to capture the segment they price out.
Gap 03 — Creator commissions
LTKShopMy
LTK: creators earn only from LTK-approved brands. ShopMy: creators earn $0 from brands not paying $999/month or enrolled in an affiliate network — no incentive to promote them.
Every designer on TF is commissionable by every creator from day one.
Gap 04 — Gamification
LTKShopMy
Both LTK and ShopMy are purely transactional — no points, challenges, leaderboards, or community mechanics. Creator retention depends entirely on commission earning; there is nothing that builds habit or community.
Community mechanics — challenges, drop leaderboards, badges — create retention and engagement neither platform offers.
Gap 05 — Virtual try-on
LTKShopMy
Neither ShopMy nor LTK has virtual try-on for creators or consumers. Both platforms show static product images only.
Virtual try-on is part of TF's documented user flow — creators preview before promoting, consumers before buying.
Gap 06 — Designer-creator matching
LTKShopMy
Neither platform algorithmically matches designers with relevant creators. Designers sign up and wait to be discovered. LTK offers a directory search; ShopMy is fully self-serve. No platform surfaces "here are creators whose audience fits your drop."
AI matching surfaces the right creators for each drop — no manual outreach required.
01 Who falls through
Designers
  • Designers with no existing creator relationships — neither platform surfaces relevant creators for their drops
  • Pre-order / limited-inventory brands (excluded by LTK policy)
  • Brands without affiliate network infrastructure on either platform
  • Designers under ~$300K–$500K revenue — LTK's minimum $5K/year upfront fee is unaffordable as a fixed cost at this scale
  • Non-US designers with no US affiliate presence
  • Capsule and made-to-order brands — model not supported
  • Drop designers who need consumer try-on to reduce pre-order hesitation — no try-on on either platform
Creators / Influencers
  • Nano-influencers under 5,000 followers (LTK non-US threshold)
  • Micro-influencers in niche subcategories — streetwear, sustainable, cultural fashion — underserved on both platforms
  • Prosumers: creators who also make or design product — neither platform is built for this dual role
  • Creators whose preferred designers are not in LTK's or ShopMy's brand network
  • Creators who want community engagement mechanics beyond commission — neither platform offers challenges, leaderboards, or social discovery
  • Creators who need virtual try-on to preview products before promoting — neither platform has this capability
  • Creators who want to be matched with relevant emerging designers — must discover and pitch brands manually on both platforms
Geography / Niche
  • LTK's brand ecosystem is US-centric; non-US creator minimum (5,000 followers) is higher than the US implied threshold
  • ShopMy has no stated geographic restriction for creators but brand coverage skews toward US-listed retailers
  • Subculture fashion niches (avant-garde, size-inclusive, cultural heritage) are not documented exclusions but are structurally under-represented
