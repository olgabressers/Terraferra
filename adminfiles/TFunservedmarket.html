TF Business Model & GTM · Research
Unserved Market Segments
Which designers and creators ShopMy and LTK do not serve, why, and how large the gap is.
Conclusion · LTK exclusions
LTK's policies structurally exclude TF's target segment.
  • LTK requires brands to have "a wide range of in-stock products" — pre-order and limited-run designers are excluded by policy (Source: LTK FAQ)
  • Creators can only link brands already inside LTK's network — independent designers without affiliate infrastructure are invisible to creators on the platform
  • Brand entry cost: $5,000–$12,000+/year — prohibitive for designers under ~$1M revenue (Source: archive.com / Capterra)
Conclusion · ShopMy limits
Lower barrier for creators, but same structural block for emerging designers.
  • Creator minimum: 1,000 followers on at least one platform (Source: ShopMy Creator Guide) — lower than LTK's 5,000 for non-US creators
  • Brands must pay ~$999/month for direct partner access OR be enrolled in a major affiliate network (ShareASale, Impact, Rakuten, CJ)
  • Independent designers without affiliate network membership cannot become commissionable partners — creators cannot earn from linking their products (Source: ShopMy Brand Guide)
TF Relevance · Structural Advantage
TF's integrated commission bypasses the affiliate network requirement entirely.
  • A designer onboards once on TF and every creator on the platform can immediately earn from driving their drops — no affiliate network enrollment required
  • Pre-order and limited-run drops are TF's core use case; LTK's "in-stock products" policy excludes this model by design
  • At $5K–$35K/year, LTK pricing is 5–35x beyond the reach of the segment TF targets — the cost gap alone creates a natural opening
Section 01
Who falls through
Designers
  • Pre-order / limited-inventory brands (excluded by LTK policy)
  • Brands without affiliate network infrastructure on either platform
  • Designers generating under ~$1M revenue (LTK brand fees prohibitive)
  • Non-US designers with no US affiliate presence
  • Capsule and made-to-order brands — model not supported
Creators / Influencers
  • Nano-influencers under 5,000 followers (LTK non-US threshold)
  • Micro-influencers in niche subcategories — streetwear, sustainable, cultural fashion — underserved on both platforms
  • Prosumers: creators who also make or design product — neither platform is built for this dual role
  • Creators whose preferred designers are not in LTK's or ShopMy's brand network
Geography / Niche
  • LTK's brand ecosystem is US-centric; non-US creator minimum (5,000 followers) is higher than the US implied threshold
  • ShopMy has no stated geographic restriction for creators but brand coverage skews toward US-listed retailers
  • Subculture fashion niches (avant-garde, size-inclusive, cultural heritage) are not documented exclusions but are structurally under-represented
