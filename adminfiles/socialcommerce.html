1Come
A stranger has no brand to follow and no creator connection yet. What makes the platform worth a first visit — beyond a product to buy?
Style tribes
Communities built around aesthetics, not brands — minimalist, streetwear, quiet luxury. A member joins the tribe that dresses like them.
InsideThe tribe's own drop feed · member looks · a style-tag library grown from names members coin for looks.

Brand space
Every brand has a permanent home on the platform: a video story, live sessions the designer holds, and a recording library. A consumer arrives through a creator's post — the brand space is the deeper pull that converts browse to follow.
InsideBrand intro video · upcoming live sessions · session recordings for community members · the brand's drop history.
Serves Consumer P1 · Brand P3 · Source: suggested
Drops as events
A drop is an appointment, not a listing: countdown, live stock counter, one window. People arrive for a moment shared with others.
InsideCountdown & live counters · "join 47 others in this drop" · sold-out cards that circulate outside the platform.
Serves Consumer P2 · Brand P4 · Source: Referral & Viral §04
2Stay
The visitor is in. What is there to do between buying — the activity that makes a session longer than a checkout?
Personal space
A private area for each member's own curation: mood boards, saved looks, style ideas, outfit plans, notes from stylist sessions. The platform becomes a home for their style thinking, not just a shop.
InsidePrivate mood boards · saved drops and looks · style notes · stylist session notes stored in one place.

Members make the look their own: one selfie → an AI video of them in the piece, or raw self-filmed content. They name the style; the best get featured and paid a bonus.
InsideThemed creation per drop · AI try-on video or amateur UGC · style naming that feeds the discovery tags.
