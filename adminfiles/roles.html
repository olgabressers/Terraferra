08. Cross-functional Notes

CEO load
4 of 6 activity groups sit with the CEO (Sales, Marketing, Operations, Legal). This is intentional for a 3-founder team at pre-launch, but is unsustainable beyond Month 6. The first hire after $10K MRR is an Operations Coordinator to absorb day-to-day ops and free the CEO to focus on Sales + Investor reporting.
Founder–CEO interface
Founder owns external capital and strategic partnerships. CEO owns execution and metrics. Friction point: investor pitch material requires CEO traction data, so the operating cadence on metrics must be tight and reliable. Weekly Founder–CEO sync on pipeline + traction.
CTO–CEO interface
Product roadmap is driven by Sales feedback (designer needs) and Operations needs (onboarding flow, support tooling). CTO and CEO sync weekly on roadmap and ticket priority. CTO does not own customer-facing communication; CEO and Marketing do.
External support coverage
Freelance roster covers what the founding team does not own directly: design refresh, marketing creative, PR launch, testing/QA, additional legal work beyond retainer. Pilot.com covers all bookkeeping and US tax. Each founder handles personal local-jurisdiction tax via the per-founder office allowance.
